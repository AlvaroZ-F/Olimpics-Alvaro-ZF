Athenas 1896

This story begins at 776 BC in Olympia, Greece, where the First Olympic Games were held. The prize for the athletes was a olive crown. Eleven centuries after, the roman emperor Teodisio I, would suspend them, considering them some pagan show. The Olympic Games in modern age were reinvented by baron Pierre de Courbertin and were held in 1894 at the Soborna University in Paris. His idea was to globalize sports under the motto "citius, altius, fortius" ("faster, higher, stronger").

With the questioning of the greeks

With the questioning of the greeks, as this country would consider the Olympic Games its property and that it had to be celebrated on their territory, the women make their public olympic debut, since 19 of them participated in Paris. The first of them to be crowned champion is the tennis player Charlotte Cooper. These are some odd Games for the wide variety of sports and its five month duration (From 24th May to 28th October), as they already became a part of the Universal exposition in the french capital.
